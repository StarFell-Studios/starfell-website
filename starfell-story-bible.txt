STARFELL: DAY ONE — STORY BIBLE
(Issues 1-3, by Mike Uhlir. Setting: StarFell, Alabama — Oct 20, 1962; frame story Oct 27.)
Compiled for visual-novel adaptation.

==== 1. PLOT BEATS IN ORDER ====
--- ISSUE 1 (pp. 3-30) ---
1. Cold open in deep space: Byron's "Darkness" captions ("I had a dream, which was not all a dream...") play over a shattered planet and a glowing pink-veined meteoroid drifting through the solar system. [p.3]
2. The meteoroid cracks apart near Earth and a burning fragment plunges into the atmosphere. [p.4]
3. 60,000 years ago: the fragment streaks over what will one day be Central Alabama, startling deer, and detonates in a blinding impact. [p.5]
4. The impact leaves a vast crater with a pulsing pink-crystal heart; the Byron captions close ("...were chill'd into a selfish prayer for light"). Cut to September 18, 1956: a crowd watches a building burn as a boy screams "DAAAD!!!" [p.6]
5. Fireman Jack Levins carries young Levi out of the road; Grandma Betty apologizes — Levi bolted when he saw his dad. Jack tells them to get home where it's safe; Betty jokes about missing the social event of the season. [p.7]
6. Jack tells Levi to mind his Grandma and stop "runnin' all over creation"; they exchange "Love you, son / Love you..." — then a woman screams that her daughter is still inside the burning building. [p.8]
7. Jack charges back into the burning building alone as strange pink crystal glows amid the flames; he kicks through a door searching for the girl. [p.9]
8. Jack finds the little blonde girl, shields her with a mattress as the ceiling collapses, and — saying "If I don't make it out of this, you better live one hell of a life for me..." — hurls them both through a window. [p.10]
9. Jack twists mid-air to cushion the girl; they hit a parked car with a THWUMP and a sickening SNAP — the girl lives, Jack's neck breaks. Levi runs toward him screaming "DAAAD!!!" [p.11]
10. Young Levi begs his dead father to wake up ("Just wake up, Dad! WAKE UP!!!") — and the scene is revealed as Levi's recurring nightmare as Grandma's voice calls him awake. [p.12]
11. October 20, 1962, "the most perfect of Saturday mornings": Grandma Betty (cooking breakfast) threatens to "pick a switch off the tree" if Levi doesn't get up; he's due at Mr. Franks' store in fifteen minutes. [p.13]
12. Breakfast banter: Betty orders a bath and laundry, quoting Judge Watkins — "If you can smell yourself, everyone else has been smelling you for three days already." [p.14]
13. Betty gives Levi a whole dollar for lunch plus milk and two pounds of hamburger meat for supper ("I expect some change!"); they trade I-love-yous and Levi bikes off down the autumn street. [p.15]
14. Title splash: Levi rides his bike across the big bridge into StarFell as the STARFELL logo appears over the river-and-foothills town. [p.16]
15. Riding through town, Levi greets Miles and Dr. Maitland outside the filling station, then arrives downtown. [p.17]
16. At Franks' Grocery, Mr. Franks chews Levi out for clocking in at 9:03 and piles on chores (sorting filthy eggs, trash, windows, mopping); a leather-jacketed Johnny Gale needles Franks for treating Levi like "a regular ol' Cinderella." [p.18]
17. Johnny buys a pulp mag and a pack of Lucky's; Franks erupts, calling him "a talking pile of trash," reveals Johnny's own daddy shot him last summer, and threatens to break his neck. Franks' son Bobby Junior pipes up — Johnny cruelly asks how about he "crawl into a whiskey bottle and die, like your momma did?" [p.19]
18. Junior swings at Johnny; Johnny decks him with one punch (THUD). Franks grabs a bat, says he's sick of Johnny stirring trouble "every damn week" and is calling the Sheriff; Johnny sneers that Junior "don't have the sand" to handle his own battles and strolls out. [p.20]
19. Junior's jaw may be broken; Franks must drive him to Doctor Ellis, so he orders Levi to deliver a grocery order to the Voltaire estate — alone. Levi protests: his Grandma told him never to set foot near that property. Franks: leave it at the front gate, talk to no one, then take the rest of the day off. [p.21]
20. Levi tows the wagon of groceries up a wooded road past a NO TRESPASSING sign and gets his first awed look at the looming, decaying Voltaire mansion ("Oh, wow!"). [p.22]
21. While unloading at the locked iron front gate, Levi turns to find a pale, dark-haired young woman silently standing behind the bars watching him — he yelps in shock. [p.23]
22. Flustered, Levi explains he works at Franks' Grocery and is just dropping the delivery; the strange girl stares, unmoving and unblinking, as he babbles his goodbyes. [p.24]
23. The gate KERCLICKS open by itself; Levi turns to flee but the girl is suddenly outside the gate, inches away — "Hi..." — and he screams and falls. [p.25]
24. The girl laughs at her prank ("You should have seen your face!"), teaches him the phrase "a roaring guffaw," and introduces herself: Delilah Voltaire, heiress to the estate. Hearing "Levins," she gleefully recalls the newspaper story — "Your daddy fell, trying to save a baby girl! Broke his neck!" [p.26]
25. Delilah blithely recaps Levi's tragedies (orphan, mother died in childbirth) then scolds him for crying. Levi tries to leave the groceries at the gate per Franks' orders; Delilah pressures him — the Voltaires own the bank that holds Grandma's mortgage — so he caves and agrees to carry the delivery in. [p.27]
26. Delilah, delighted to finally have a guest, leads Levi through the gates and presents the mansion: "Welcome to Fleur Du Ciel!" — bats circling its spire above an overgrown topiary garden. [p.28]
27. Two miles south of StarFell, a lone fisherman sings "In the Pines" by a dark lake ringed with pink-glowing crystal; a crystal-infested roach-thing bites his neck and infection erupts over his body. [p.29]
28. The fisherman screams for help as he mutates (SPLUTCH) into a spiny crystal-spider monstrosity that lurches toward the buildings of town, croaking "In the pines... in the pines..." [p.30]

--- ISSUE 2 (pp. 32-55) ---
29. Frame story, one week later (Oct 27): in a barricaded hideout, a battered Johnny Gale helps young Lina Jane cut monster pictures (a dragon, a lion) into her red scrapbook before the group moves out — "Okay! World Shakers!" [p.32]
30. Splash, October 27, 1962: Johnny ("Let's go shake the world.") leads the surviving kids — Levi, Miles, Dash, Lucas, Kristy, Lina Jane and others — in a wrecked, tentacle-strewn lodge. [p.33]
31. Johnny arms up and reviews the plan; outside, the kids find the building they need surrounded by a horde of crystal-mutated monsters under bat-things and floating discs — "Crap." [p.34]
32. A bat-gargoyle creature SKREEs through a window; Johnny yells "Everybody, get down!", shoots it off Miles (BLAM/SKLURTCH) and checks "Miles! Are you okay?" [p.35]
33. Flashback to Oct 20 — "MILES...": Miles and Dr. Maitland hike the ridge, trading jokes about the hundred pounds of gear and the doctor's mock-heroic quest speech (a "perilous quest" for the Alabama Geological Society). [p.36]
34. Father and son crest the ridge: the astrobleme — Dr. Maitland's joy is "rocks, and one big damn hole in the ground," a rare above-ground impact crater, "a literal gift from the heavens"; pink crystals jut from its floor as they descend to camp. [p.37]
35. Back at Fleur Du Ciel, Delilah rhapsodizes about autumn's "most perfect little things" (crisp air, pecan trees, the sky's "soft, pink twilight hue," a knife plunged into a pumpkin); Levi interrupts to ask where the groceries go — "Horace will come and take them away." [p.38]
36. Delilah notes the spider lilies blooming, demands Levi's favorite "fleur d'automne," names hers — the pink lady's slipper orchid ("You might want to write that down") — and abruptly compliments his hair before declaring "Time for the grand tour! Come, wander with me!" [p.39]
37. Garden tour: Delilah recounts Voltaire history — French gunpowder merchants who fled the Revolution, supplied America's Manifest Destiny ("Every bullet and cannon fired during the Creek War was done so using OUR gunpowder"), bought up southern land, and shipped the chateau over from France piece by piece. [p.40]
38. Delilah defends the Voltaires as philanthropists ("the town likes to paint us as the villains"), reveals an unsettling habit of spying on all of StarFell from the belvedere bell tower and speaks of "my pets and the myriad of spirits that keep me company"; in the garden below sits her father Alistair in his wheelchair. [p.41]
39. Delilah introduces Levi to "Daddy"; Alistair eyes the offered hand, glares, and spits on Levi's sneaker (SPPFFT/SPLAT). [p.42]
40. Delilah scolds her father; the hulking butler Horace pries the spit-stained shoe situation under control, apologizes for Alistair ("He's getting to an age of confusion... sometimes he lashes out for no reason"), and Alistair clamps onto his hand until Horace is freed. [p.43]
41. At the crater camp, Miles examines a pink crystal through his loupe-goggles; Dr. Maitland notes the purple color might mean iron but "what is EXTRAordinary is the origin of the crystal itself" — while an unseen crystal-eyed creature watches them. [p.44]
42. Levi tries to leave; Delilah asks "Have... have YOU ever gone fishing?" then suddenly SNAPS off a lock of his hair with scissors — "OOOWW!!! What the--?!?" [p.45]
43. Delilah insists Levi needs "a trim," chasing him with scissors ("I've only practiced on my dolls and the cat"); a stern woman in purple appears: "Let the boy go... DELILAH!" — her older sister Lilith. [p.46]
44. At the crater, Miles wires the coil emitters and amplifier; Dr. Maitland lets him turn the dial — the crystals crackle (BZZZAAK) and Miles realizes they're converting the gamma waves into a different form of energy; they push the dial to seven. [p.47]
45. The sisters fight: Delilah ("This is MY friend and WE want to go INSIDE and play BARBERSHOP!") vs Lilith ("Nobody wants to be your friend, Lilith... you're a wretched, old HAG" — answered by "your heart is as BARREN as your womb"); Lilith SMACKS Delilah, who shrieks "I am sick of the family treating me like Rapunzel... I AM NO RAPUNZEL!" and slams inside. [p.48]
46. At the crater the experiment overloads — "That's TOO MUCH! Turn it off!" "I JUST DID!" — and a massive pink shockwave BOOOOMs out of the astrobleme, throwing Dr. Maitland down as Miles screams "DAD!" [p.49]
47. Lilith inspects Levi's bleeding ear, hands him fifty dollars in hush money, and coaches him to say he fell off his bike onto a sharp rock — "if any word GETS OUT about what transpired here, it will result in MORE than a pained EAR." The distant BOOOOM interrupts; she sends him off: "Make haste, and REMEMBER to keep your mouth shut." [p.50]
48. Aftermath at the crater: Dr. Maitland, shaken, mutters "I fear all we have done is to awaken a sleeping giant and fill him with a terrible resolve"; he sends Miles home alone via the shortcut to StarFell proper while he stays to clean up. [p.51]
49. Ten minutes later Miles and Levi meet on the trail and swap stories — Levi's "sharp rock" lie flops; Miles describes the shockwave; arriving in town they find the street wrecked and smoking: "I-- I think MY DAD and I happened." [p.52]
50. Back at the crater Dr. Maitland packs up; three figures loom on the ridge above him — Horace, Alistair in his chair, and Lilith — and as he greets them a clawed root-monster bursts up and seizes him mid-sentence ("--WHA--? AAAHHHHHH!!!"). [p.53]
51. Dusk, a quiet house: humming "In the pines, in the pines, where the sun never shines," the fisherman-monster creeps onto a porch and up to a child's bedroom window. [p.54]
52. Inside, little Lina Jane plays with her cat and her magical glowing scrapbook (a cut-out cat picture shimmers with pink sparkles); her mom calls her to supper, unaware of the thing at the window. [p.55]

--- ISSUE 3 (pp. 57-82) ---
53. Morning at Fleur Du Ciel (earlier on Oct 20): Delilah wakes in a bedroom crammed with teddy bears, dresses, and twirls singing "I'm Forever Blowing Bubbles" with live bats tying her sash like fairy-tale birds. [p.57]
54. Still singing, Delilah breezes past Lilith, Alistair, and Horace at the breakfast table, swiping an apple, then climbs to the belvedere with her telescope — "Good morning, handsome..." — spying on someone far below. [p.58]
55. The telescope's target: Johnny Gale, smoking at the wheel of his black hot rod, roaring down a country road (October 20, 1962). [p.59]
56. Johnny delivers a basket of moonshine ("a cure-all... for misery and depression," with apples for vitamin C) to old Mister Charles, whose ruined, paper-strewn house Johnny gently chides — "Jeez, Charles... you really let this place go." [p.60]
57. Mister Charles's flowers monologue: since Darlene died last year he's "just been existing"; his biggest regret was never bringing her enough flowers though "they grow ALL OVER the property... Now all I want to do is go back in time and plow a whole damn field for her. But we can't go back, can we, Johnny?" Johnny: "No, sir, unfortunately we cannot," refuses payment ("aren't you an angel? ...Johnny Angel"), then car doors SLAM outside. [p.61]
58. The Sheriff and Deputy Kyle confront Johnny in the yard, reading off the morning's complaints — speeding, sneaking out Mr. Woods' daughter's window at six, into Ms. Bailey's daughter's window at six-thirty — and breaking Bobby Junior's jaw at the grocery; Johnny quips the invitations were "personal" and Junior's "piss-poor attitude was definitely inviting." [p.62]
59. The Sheriff arrests Johnny anyway — the Franks family has money and "those with the most money make the most NOISE" — but offers a deal: ride back to town in his own car (Deputy Kyle following), get booked, see Judge Watkins Monday, car impounded so he can't skip town; Charles offers to keep the 'shine. [p.63]
60. At the Sheriff's office, Johnny and the mocking Deputy Kyle ("this little piggy went all the way home") nearly come to blows — then the crater shockwave hits town, blowing in the windows ("Everyone, get down!!!" / RRUUUUMMMMBBL). [p.64]
61. Chaos downtown: the Sheriff sends Kyle for emergency services and drafts Johnny ("Johnny, you come with ME!"); they lift a fallen beam off a car as Mr. Woods — the same man whose daughter's window Johnny exited — pointedly doesn't recognize him. [p.65]
62. Johnny brings ice to an injured man; the Sheriff approves ("If you can't assist your fellow man in times of need, then I reckon what good ARE you?"), releases him for now ("I STILL want you in front of the judge come NINE on Monday"). Johnny: "I'll be there with bells on." [p.66]
63. Levi and Miles survey the wrecked street (a light pole through Stubb's Ice Cream Shop); Miles frets about blame; Dash and Lucas Jackson run up — the brothers saw the bright pink light by the river and got flung ashore by the waves, and the "museum lady," Missus Sharon, saw the blast come from the hills with the big hole. [p.67]
64. The boys swap cover stories ("Accidentally slid down a hill" / "Fell out of a tree"); Grandma's car screeches up — "Get your BUTT in this car RIGHT NOW!" — she's spent thirty minutes fearing Levi was dead under rubble. [p.68]
65. Levi begs to stay with the fellas; Grandma refuses; his bike falls against Johnny's leg, and Johnny advises him to be nicer to his grandma: "It's better to give her flowers while she is HERE, instead of bringing them to her when she's GONE." [p.69]
66. Ambush: Bobby Junior (jaw wired shut) and three burly redheaded kin corner Johnny — "You and I got unfinished business!"; Johnny mocks the broken jaw, and they promise to "stomp your face in." [p.70]
67. Johnny flicks his cigarette in one cousin's eye and fights hard (KAFRUMPH, SMACK), but four on one is too many — they pull a bat as Junior orders "Hold him still, dammit!" [p.71]
68. KRAK — Johnny is beaten with the bat ("Puh-pu-p-please don't..."), curb-stomped by the gang ("Get him, boys!"), and stuffed unconscious into a car trunk: "Put that sack of white trash in the TRUNK before anyone sees us!" [p.72]
69. Miles gets home to his worried mother; Dr. Maitland is "still at the test site, packing up." Mom leaves to check on unreachable Nana, telling Miles his father should be home shortly; TV dinners in the freezer. [p.73]
70. Night: the Franks gang dumps Johnny at the Old Calaboose — StarFell's first jail, built 1820, where "they say you could hear a prisoner scream from miles away... as he was gettin' eaten alive by all types of critters" — and throws him into the cell (THUD), bricking the entrance shut, laughing about the cold front coming. [p.74]
71. At Lina Jane's house, mother and daughter watch a GROM monster movie ("We didn't make it in time-- GROM is here!"); Mom dozes off after double shifts; Lina Jane has seen the movie five times — "It's not scary to me anymore." Goodnights and I-love-yous; church in the morning. [p.75]
72. Late night: Miles wakes at his desk; his dad never came home ("Mom? Dad? Anybody here?"). He packs a lantern, straps on his homemade gauntlet gadget, and slips out the window: "I hope you're okay... I'll find you, Dad." [p.76]
73. Lina Jane wakes to a CRASH; the fisherman-monster has broken in and holds her unconscious mother in its claws, hungrily growling "POWER." [p.77]
74. Tentacles seize Lina Jane; the monster rasps "Ehh... innn... IN THE PINES" as her eyes glow pink — her scrapbook flares with pink light and something huge materializes behind the page ("GROM is un-stop-pable!"). [p.78]
75. The movie robot GROM, summoned from Lina Jane's scrapbook, blasts the monster apart (BZZZAAAP) and pounds the remains. [p.79]
76. Lina Jane crawls out to a smoking carcass; she shyly touches the kneeling robot's hand ("Are you really...?" "GROM!"), then — realizing — runs screaming into the night for her mom while GROM watches over the body. [p.80]
77. Miles searches the dark crater trail ("Where ARE you, Dad?") and spots green, pink-eyed lizard-men in yellow uniforms carrying his unconscious (or dead) father into a crate-like container. [p.81]
78. A white-haired man in a black suit catches Miles — "Who are you?" / "NOT TODAY!" — Miles bolts, but with a gesture the man animates the vines (THWIP, SKRITCH), which bind Miles head to foot. Cliffhanger; end of Issue 3. [p.82]
(p.83 is a pinup: "StarFell Studios" — a suited woman carrying a tentacled briefcase.)

==== 2. LOCATIONS ====
DEEP SPACE / SOLAR SYSTEM — Shattered alien planet and drifting debris field; a pink-crystal-veined meteoroid travels past the planets toward Earth. Timeless cosmic night. Tone: cosmic wonder shading into dread. [pp.3-4]

PREHISTORIC ALABAMA WILDERNESS — Rolling green forested hills, pond, deer; "what will one day be known as Central Alabama," 60,000 years ago. Golden daylight obliterated by the impact fireball. Tone: pastoral serenity destroyed in an instant. [pp.5-6]

THE ASTROBLEME / CRATER — Vast above-ground impact crater in the foothills ridges south of town; rocky bowl studded with jutting pink-purple crystal; Maitland test camp (tents, amplifier, coil emitters) on its floor. Day (golden afternoon) and night. Tone: scientific wonder turning to dread — the "sleeping giant." [pp.37, 44, 47, 49, 51, 53, 81-82]

BURNING SCHOOL/BUILDING, DOWNTOWN STARFELL (1956) — Brick multi-story building ablaze at night, crowd in the street, pink crystal glowing inside the firestruck rooms; the blue parked car where Jack lands. Night. Tone: chaos, heroism, grief. [pp.6-12]

LEVINS HOUSE — Modest two-story home with porch, front yard tree, checkered-floor kitchen with radio; Grandma's domain. Saturday morning, warm light. Tone: warm, loving, gently nagging domesticity. [pp.13-15]

THE BRIDGE — Long lamp-lined river bridge into StarFell, autumn foliage, church steeple and crater-hill visible beyond. Morning. Tone: wonder, Americana freedom. [p.16]

DOWNTOWN STARFELL — Main-street storefronts, filling station, Stubb's Ice Cream Shop, barbershop; later strewn with smashed glass, downed poles and wires after the shockwave. Day. Tone: idyllic small-town bustle, then chaotic disaster. [pp.17, 52, 65-68]

FRANKS' GROCERY — Cramped general store: register counter, shelves, crates of eggs in back. Morning. Tone: petty tyranny, comic friction, sudden violence. [pp.18-21]

WOODED ROAD / VOLTAIRE GATE — Rutted dirt lane past a NO TRESPASSING sign, dark overgrown trees, high stone wall and wrought-iron front gate. Day, but shadowed. Tone: eerie, forbidden, quiet dread. [pp.22-25]

FLEUR DU CIEL (VOLTAIRE ESTATE) — Decaying French Second-Empire mansion shipped from France piece by piece; belvedere bell tower with telescope, bat swarms, statuary, hedge gardens ("Les Grands Jardins de la Fleur Du Ciel") of exotic carnivorous-looking plants, spider lilies; Delilah's teddy-bear-crammed bedroom; formal breakfast room; spiral stair. Day/dusk; interiors perpetually dim. Tone: gothic faded grandeur — whimsy with menace underneath. [pp.28, 38-43, 45-48, 50, 57-58]

THE LAKE, TWO MILES SOUTH OF STARFELL ("IN THE PINES") — Dark pine-ringed fishing lake, shores studded with glowing pink crystal growths and infected insects. Dusk gloom. Tone: pure horror. [pp.29-30]

COUNTRY ROAD / MISTER CHARLES'S HOUSE — Backwoods lane (squirrels, hot rod); Charles's sagging wooden house, trash-strewn rooms, porch — kept up by his late wife Darlene, now derelict. Morning. Tone: melancholy warmth, grief and regret. [pp.59-61, 63]

SHERIFF'S OFFICE — Small-town station: desks, coffee pot, big windows (blown in by the shockwave). Midday. Tone: needling banter cut short by disaster. [p.64]

THE OLD CALABOOSE — StarFell's first jail, built 1820, an isolated stone blockhouse where prisoners were "eaten alive by critters"; cell with barred window, entrance bricked shut. Cold night. Tone: claustrophobic horror, cruelty. [p.74, echoed by Issue 3 cover p.56]

MAITLAND HOUSE — Tidy middle-class home; Miles's bunk-bed room with workbench, gadget wall, clock; living room. Evening/night. Tone: warm but increasingly anxious and empty. [pp.73, 76]

LINA JANE'S HOUSE — Small one-story house with porch; her toy-filled bedroom, living room with TV and couch. Night. Tone: cozy innocence violated by horror, then storybook wonder (GROM). [pp.54-55, 75, 77-80]

CRATER TRAIL / WOODS AT NIGHT — Moonlit rocky switchback trail and vine-choked woods between town and the astrobleme. Night. Tone: lonely dread, then capture-panic. [pp.76, 81-82]

WORLD SHAKERS HQ (FRAME STORY, OCT 27) — Wrecked, barricaded lodge/hall: boarded windows, hanging lamps, severed tentacles on the floor, a caged pink creature; monster-besieged civic building visible outside under floating discs. Perpetual besieged dusk. Tone: weary resolve, last-stand camaraderie. [pp.32-35]


==== 3. CHARACTERS ====
LEVI LEVINS — Protagonist; ~12-year-old orphan in red/black striped shirt, works at Franks' Grocery, lives with Grandma. First app: Issue 1, p.7 (as younger boy in 1956 flashback; 1962-present from p.12). Key actions: witnesses father Jack's death in 1956 (recurring nightmare); delivers groceries to the Voltaire estate; befriended/terrorized by Delilah (hair snipped, ear cut); bribed and threatened into silence by Lilith; lies to Miles, swaps stories with the gang downtown; collected by Grandma after the shockwave; among the World Shakers in the Oct 27 frame story.

GRANDMA (BETTY) — Levi's grandmother and guardian; sharp-tongued, loving. First app: Issue 1, p.7. Key actions: minds Levi at the 1956 fire; runs the Levins household with switch-tree threats and maxims; sends Levi to work with lunch money and a supper list; forbade him ever to go near the Voltaire property; frantically retrieves him after the shockwave.

JACK LEVINS — Levi's father, StarFell fireman. First app: Issue 1, p.7. Key actions: carries Levi from the road at the 1956 fire; tells him to mind Grandma ("Love you, son"); re-enters the burning building to save a trapped little girl; throws them both from a window, shielding her, and dies of a broken neck on a car roof. (The girl he saved is strongly implied to be significant later.)

JOHNNY GALE — Town greaser/bad-boy with a heart of gold; black hot rod, leather jacket. First app: Issue 1, p.18. Key actions: defends Levi from Franks's bullying; one-punches Bobby Junior; delivers moonshine to lonely Mister Charles and refuses payment ("Johnny Angel"); arrested by the Sheriff, then helps rescue townsfolk after the shockwave; advises Levi about flowers for Grandma; ambushed by the Franks boys, beaten with a bat, and entombed in the Old Calaboose; by Oct 27 he leads the kids as the "World Shakers."

MILES MAITLAND — Levi's friend; gadget-genius kid with goggles and homemade gauntlet. First app: Issue 1, p.17 (featured from Issue 2, p.36). Key actions: hikes to the astrobleme with his dad; wires the amplifier experiment that triggers the shockwave; meets Levi on the trail; goes home to an empty house, then sneaks out at night to find his father — sees lizard-men carrying him off and is captured by the vine-controlling man in black. In the frame story he's saved from a bat-creature by Johnny.

DR. MAITLAND — Miles's father; geologist for the Alabama Geological Society, warm and playfully theatrical. First app: Issue 1, p.17. Key actions: leads the crater expedition; runs the gamma-wave experiment on the crystals; knocked down by the shockwave; utters the "sleeping giant" line; stays to clean up, is attacked by a root-monster while Horace, Alistair and Lilith watch from the ridge, and is later carried off unconscious by lizard-men.

LINA JANE — Small blonde girl with a magical scrapbook and an orange cat. First app: Issue 2, p.32 (frame story; Oct 20 from p.54). Key actions: cuts out monster/creature pictures that glow with pink magic; watches the GROM movie with her exhausted mom; attacked by the fisherman-monster; her power manifests, summoning GROM from the scrapbook to destroy it; runs for help. In the frame story she preps her scrapbook (dragon, lion) for battle.

LINA JANE'S MOM — Tired, loving single(?) working mother. First app: Issue 2, p.55 [voice]; on-panel Issue 3, p.75. Key actions: double shifts; movie night with Lina Jane; seized unconscious by the fisherman-monster; fate left uncertain.

DASH JACKSON — Blond pompadoured kid, bubble-gum, gum-pink confidence; Lucas's brother. First app: Issue 2, p.33 (Oct 20: Issue 3, p.67). Key actions: with Lucas, flung ashore by the river waves from the shockwave; reports Missus Sharon's account; joins the World Shakers.

LUCAS JACKSON — Dash's younger(?) brother. First app: Issue 2, p.33 (Oct 20: Issue 3, p.67). Key actions: greets Levi and Miles downtown, swears secrecy about "the meteor"; recounts the pink light at the river; World Shaker.

KRISTY LEIGH — Blonde girl in red jacket, ponytail. First app: Issue 2, p.33 (with the World Shakers; also p.34 with sword/bat). Key actions: member of Johnny's frame-story crew.

DELILAH VOLTAIRE — Childlike, unsettling heiress of Fleur Du Ciel; black bob, green dress, scissors. First app: Issue 1, p.23. Key actions: stalks and pranks Levi at the gate; recites his family tragedies with gleeful tactlessness; pressures him inside (the bank threat); gives the garden tour and family history; spies on the whole town from the belvedere ("Good morning, handsome..." at Johnny); snips Levi's hair and tries to force a "barbershop" haircut; fights with sister Lilith ("I am no Rapunzel!"); sings show tunes while bats dress her.

LILITH VOLTAIRE — Delilah's elder sister; severe, purple suit, the family enforcer. First app: Issue 2, p.46. Key actions: stops Delilah's scissors attack; slaps her down ("your heart is as barren as your womb" exchange); pays Levi $50 hush money with a velvet-gloved threat; watches from the ridge as Dr. Maitland is taken.

ALISTAIR VOLTAIRE — The wheelchair-bound, goateed Voltaire patriarch. First app: Issue 2, p.41. Key actions: spits on Levi's shoe instead of shaking hands; crushes Horace's hand; present on the ridge when Dr. Maitland is attacked. Delilah quotes "Daddy" for the family's gunpowder history.

HORACE — The Voltaires' immense, silent, white-haired butler/caretaker. First app: Issue 2, p.43 (named p.38). Key actions: cleans Levi's shoe; apologizes for Alistair; hauls the groceries; stands with the family on the crater ridge. Possibly the man in black who animates the vines and captures Miles [pp.81-82 — figure is white-haired, suited].

MR. FRANKS — Bald, mustachioed owner of Franks' Grocery; bully boss. First app: Issue 1, p.18. Key actions: berates Levi for being 3 minutes late; feuds violently with Johnny (reveals Johnny's father shot him last summer); sends Levi alone to the Voltaire estate; drives Junior to Doctor Ellis.
