## 1. Series Synopsis

Sixty thousand years before the first church bell rang over Central Alabama, something fell out of the sky. It did not die. The meteor that carved the great crater outside the river town of StarFell carried a core of pink crystal — a thing that sleeps, converts, and waits. For generations the town grew up around the wound in the earth: a postcard-pretty main street, a concrete bridge over the Coosa-like river, eggs at Franks' Grocery, sermons on Sunday. October 20, 1962 is "the most perfect of Saturday mornings." It is also Day One.

When a well-meaning scientist runs current through the crater's crystals, he wakes a sleeping giant and fills it with terrible resolve. A shockwave rolls over StarFell and the town's natural history turns against it — fishermen mutate mid-song into clawed things croaking "In the Pines," the river's legendary giant catfish "Fat Lance" grows tentacles, the endangered local snail swells into a monster, and pale ghosts drift down Main Street at noon. Above it all, in a transplanted French chateau called Fleur Du Ciel, sits the Voltaire family — the town's ancient, moneyed landowners, whose lineage is not of this world and whose patriarch has waited a very long time for the meteor to wake up.

Against this stands a handful of kids with homemade weapons and nothing left to lose: an orphan with his father's courage and a meteor-infused BB gun, a greaser the town wrote off as trash, a twelve-year-old prodigy who blames himself for all of it, and a little girl whose scrapbook now makes pictures come true. StarFell is a story about human resilience and youthful camaraderie in the dark — about what a small town does when the sky finally answers back, and what kids do when the adults can't save them.

## 2. Character Profiles

### The Kids ("The World Shakers")

**Levi Levins** — *The Heart. Protagonist, age 12.*
Levi carries the weight of profound loss: his mother died in childbirth and his father Jack, a StarFell firefighter, died on September 18, 1956 saving a baby girl from a burning building — leaping from a window with her in his arms and breaking his neck on a parked car. Raised by his grandmother, Levi relives that day in his dreams. Tender-hearted but stubborn, with grief that occasionally flashes into bursts of anger, he delivers groceries for Franks' Grocery — a job that sends him through the gates of the Voltaire estate on Day One. He carries his trusty Meteor Infused Red Ryder BB Gun and is determined to defend his town. Visual: shaggy brown hair, red-and-navy striped tee, jeans, black Chucks; later a leather aviator cap and goggles.

**Johnny Gale** — *The Rebel turned Leader. Teenage greaser.*
The town menace with a hot rod, a switchblade comb, and a rap sheet of broken curfews — and the truest moral compass in StarFell. His mother drank herself to death; his father shot him last summer. The "moonshine run" he's blamed for is actually a delivery of comfort to a lonely widower. When the invasion hits, Johnny's defiance becomes heroism: he pulls men from rubble, takes in the town's kids, and forges them into the World Shakers. "Okay! World Shakers! ... Let's go shake the world." Romantic tension: Delilah Voltaire watches him through her telescope every morning. Visual: black leather jacket, white tee, pompadour, Lucky Strikes, black '46 hot rod.

**Miles Maitland** — *The Brain. Prodigy, age 12.*
Son of scientist Dr. Maitland, Miles arms the youth of StarFell with gadgets and weapons forged in the crucible of his genius — including his homemade high-tech gauntlet. He helped his father run the amplifier experiment at the astrobleme that woke the meteor, and the guilt of it shadows everything he does. When his father is taken — first by a mud-creature at the crater, then by the Voltaires' green-skinned servants — Miles straps on the gauntlet and goes into the woods alone: "I hope you're okay... I'll find you, Dad." Best friend to Levi. Visual: Black, glasses, orange-and-blue chevron sweater.

**Lina Jane** — *The Wildcard. Age 7.*
A small girl with an exhausted mother, a cat, a love of monster movies, and a scrapbook that flares with pink light. After the shockwave, whatever Lina Jane pastes into the scrapbook can be conjured into the world — demonstrated the night the "In the Pines" monster broke into her home and she brought the movie robot GROM to life to blast it apart ("Grom is unstoppable!"). Grom now shadows her as a child-sized companion. Note: the pitch names her **Lina Jane Prescott**; one asset file is named "Lina Jane Hood" — confirm canonical surname with Mike before publishing. Visual: small blonde girl in a green hooded cloak, clutching the spell-book-like scrapbook.

**Dash Jackson** — *The Scout.*
Elder of the Jackson brothers — a flinty, freckled redhead in a badge-covered scout vest, built for woodcraft, first-aid, and not flinching. In Issue 4 he and his brother Lucas come face to face with the mutated catfish. Visual: red flat-top, scout vest with patches, rolled jeans, white Chucks.

**Lucas Jackson** — *The Little Brother.*
Dash's younger brother — a stocky, gap-toothed kid in overalls and a ball cap who hauls home-built weaponry (crossbow, gear, gadgets) like a pack mule and refuses to be left behind.

**Kristy Leigh** — *The Runaway. Age 16.*
A turbulent escape from a life of crime strands her in StarFell mid-invasion (her full arrival story lands in Issue 5). Haunted by a troubled past, hardened beyond her years, she fights the mutant terror alongside the World Shakers. Visual: blonde ponytail, pink bomber jacket with black racing stripes, saddle shoes — '60s tough-girl.

### The Voltaires (House Fleur Du Ciel)

**Alistair Voltaire** — *The Patriarch. Primary antagonist.*
An ancient alien wearing the life of a Southern land baron. The Voltaires have held the land "one hundred and fifty years, or so," built on a French gunpowder fortune that armed the Creek War, and they own the bank that holds half the town's mortgages — including Levi's grandma's. Weathered by time and confined (publicly) to a wheelchair, Alistair hungers to harness the meteor's energy to reshape Earth in the image of his home world and restore his own youth. The wheelchair is theater: at the end of Issue 3 he stands, walks, and commands living vines to snare Miles. He spits on Levi's shoe rather than shake an Earth child's hand.

**Delilah Voltaire** — *The Wildcard Heiress.*
StarFell's most charming nightmare: a pale, raven-haired young woman who teleports, talks to spirits, keeps live bats like pets, sings "I'm Forever Blowing Bubbles" through gloomy family breakfasts, and collects locks of hair with scissors and zero warning ("I've only practiced on my dolls and the cat"). She knows everything about everyone — including exactly how Levi's daddy died. Torn between her family's dark allure and a rebellious affection for Johnny Gale, her manic energy makes her an erratic, unpredictable ally to the children. Screams the family's quiet part out loud: "I am sick of the family treating me like Rapunzel!"

**Lilith Voltaire** — *The Operator.*
The cold, calculating daughter who manages the family's dark dealings with merciless precision — hush money, threats, and collateral damage be damned. More at ease with mutants and aliens than with human warmth, she aims to usurp unseen thrones and rule through dominance and despair. It's Lilith who presses fifty dollars into Levi's hand: "If any word gets out about what transpired here, it will result in more than a pained ear." Visual: severe gray suit, black hair, perpetual contempt; the book's lone back-matter pinup shows her carrying a wooden briefcase with tentacles spilling out.

**Horace** — *The Gardener.*
The Voltaires' hulking groundskeeper at Les Grands Jardins de la Fleur Du Ciel — a gentle giant with dirty bare feet who cleans Alistair's spit off Levi's shoe and apologizes for his master. One of the only warm presences on the estate; how much he knows is an open question.

### Supporting Cast

**Dr. (Moses) Maitland** — Miles' father; the scientist whose Alabama Geological Society survey of the astrobleme ("a literal gift from the heavens") triggers Day One. Quotes Yamamoto as the dust settles: "I fear all we have done is to awaken a sleeping giant and fill him with a terrible resolve." Abducted at the crater; the Voltaires want his mind to build them a device. (Note: a live-action asset is labeled "Moses Maitland, age 16" in a letterman jacket — confirm with Mike whether Moses is Dr. Maitland's first name, a flashback design, or a third Maitland.)

**The Sheriff** — StarFell's even-handed lawman; arrests Johnny because "those with the most money make the most noise," then releases him after watching him save lives. Decency with a badge.

**Grandma (Betty)** — Levi's guardian; flinty Southern warmth and Judge Watkins maxims: "If you can smell yourself, everyone else has been smelling you for three days already."

**Grom** — A B-movie robot pulled off the late-night creature feature and into reality by Lina Jane's scrapbook. Giant when summoned, child-sized between battles.

### The Monsters

**Fat Lance** — The final boss: StarFell's mythological giant catfish — every Southern town has one — mutated by the crystals into a tentacled, frog-legged kaiju that can walk on land. Based on Wetumpka's real river lore.
**The "In the Pines" Fisherman** — A crystal-bitten fisherman fused into a clawed horror that croaks the folk song he died singing. The series' signature dread image.
**The Mutant River Snail** — Wetumpka's real endangered Tulotoma snail, grown monstrous (full turnaround sheet exists in assets).
**Tentacled horrors, gargoyle-bats, ghosts, mud-creatures, and the Voltaires' green-skinned, yellow-jumpsuited servants** round out the bestiary.

## 3. Issue Summaries — StarFell: Day One #1–3

### Issue 1 — "Welcome to StarFell" (TPB pp. 3–30)
Opens in deep space with Lord Byron's "Darkness" as a meteor with a pink crystal core falls to prehistoric Alabama, 60,000 years ago. Smash-cut to September 18, 1956: Levi's firefighter father Jack dies saving a baby girl from a burning building whose basement, we glimpse, bristles with meteor crystals. Levi wakes from the recurring dream on October 20, 1962 — a perfect Saturday. Small-town warmth follows: Grandma, breakfast, Franks' Grocery, and Johnny Gale breaking Bobby Junior's jaw over a slur about his dead mother. Franks punishes Levi with the delivery nobody wants: groceries to the Voltaire estate. At the gates of Fleur Du Ciel, Delilah Voltaire materializes — charming, omniscient, and cruel — and pulls him inside. Cliffhanger: two miles south, a fisherman singing "In the Pines" is bitten by a crystal-mutated insect and transforms into a clawed monster shambling toward town.

### Issue 2 — "Awaken a Sleeping Giant" (TPB pp. 32–55)
Cold open one week later (October 27): Johnny leads the armed World Shakers — Levi, Miles, Dash, Kristy Leigh — through a monster-infested StarFell. Then the issue rewinds to retell October 20 from new angles. Miles and Dr. Maitland survey the astrobleme, "one of the rarest above-ground impact craters in the world," and wire an amplifier into crystals that convert gamma waves into something else. Levi gets the Fleur Du Ciel grand tour and 150 years of Voltaire lore; Delilah snips off a lock of his hair before Lilith intervenes and the sisters tear into each other. Miles cranks the amplifier — the crater erupts in a pink shockwave. Lilith buys Levi's silence with $50; Maitland quotes Yamamoto; Levi and Miles walk home through wreckage and drifting ghosts ("I think my dad and I happened"). Triple cliffhanger: a mud-creature seizes Dr. Maitland as the Voltaires watch from the crater rim; the "In the Pines" monster reaches a house at night; inside, little Lina Jane's scrapbook flares with pink magic.

### Issue 3 — "Day One" (TPB pp. 57–82)
Johnny Gale's October 20. The "moonshine run" the town judges him for is a kindness — free whiskey for the widower Mister Charles, who gives Johnny (and the book) its heart: "All I want to do is go back in time and plow a whole damn field of flowers for her. But we can't go back, can we, Johnny?" The Sheriff arrests Johnny for Bobby Junior's jaw just before the shockwave hits; Johnny spends the aftermath pulling survivors from rubble and is released. Bobby Junior's revenge: a bat to the skull and Johnny chained inside the Old Calaboose, StarFell's 1820 jail. Meanwhile, night falls: the "In the Pines" monster smashes into Lina Jane's home and grabs her mother — Lina Jane's eyes blaze pink and she conjures the movie robot GROM to blast it apart. Final cliffhanger: searching the woods for his father, Miles finds green aliens in yellow jumpsuits carrying Dr. Maitland — and Alistair Voltaire standing, walking, very much not infirm. Vines snare Miles. End of Day One.

### Teasers on file for Issues 4–6 (from the pitch — usable as "Coming Soon" copy)
**#4:** The Voltaires pressure Dr. Maitland and Miles to exploit the meteor; Dash and Lucas confront a fearsome mutated catfish; a chapter of Delilah's concealed past opens for Levi. **#5:** Johnny besieged in the ancient jail by mutant snails, with 7-year-old Lina Jane as his unexpected ally; 16-year-old runaway Kristy Leigh crashes into StarFell mid-invasion. **#6:** Levi, Lucas, and Dash raid the Voltaire estate's maze of traps and ghosts to rescue Miles; Dr. Maitland faces a cruel choice — build the Voltaires a device of immense power to save his son.

## 4. Tone & Aesthetic Direction

**The feel:** Norman Rockwell's Saturday morning, ten seconds before the sky splits. 1962 small-town Alabama — bikes, BB guns, grocery deliveries, church manners, moonshine, greasers — colliding with golden-age sci-fi horror: tentacles, ghosts, mutants, and a family of aliens living in the antebellum mansion everyone's too polite to ask about. Heart + horror + humor, in that order. It's a horror book a kid is allowed to read: real dread, real grief (dead parents are the engine of this story), no vulgarity.

**Touchstones:** *The Goonies*, *Stranger Things*, *The Sandlot*, Lovecraft, 1950s–60s creature features (*The Blob*, *Them!*, *It Came From Outer Space*), Southern Gothic. The literary frame is Byron's "Darkness"; the musical frame is the murder ballad "In the Pines."

**Design language for the site:**
- **Palette:** Warm autumn Americana (rust, mustard, faded teal, cream paper) violated by the meteor's **pink/magenta glow**. Pink = the cosmic wrongness; use it for accents, hovers, glows, monster sections. Deep night-blue/black for horror sections. Reference `Concept Art/Final/crater.jpg` (pink glow rising from dark crater) — that image IS the color story.
- **Typography:** 1960s retro display type. The StarFell logo is an angled, beveled script-deco hybrid (pink). Pair with a clean grotesque or a period serif for body. Pulp "HORROR comics" cover (pitch p. 21 / EC-Comics pastiche) is license to use distressed pulp textures sparingly.
- **Texture:** Halftone dots, paper grain, postcard borders ("Welcome to StarFell" / "Thanks for Stopping By!" postcard art exists and should bookend the site — hero and footer).
- **Motion (restrained):** Slow pink pulse on crystal/glow elements; drifting fog; nothing bouncy. Dread is slow.
- **Voice for site copy:** Southern, warm, wry, then unsettling. Write like a town welcome brochure that occasionally says something terrifying ("Population 4,212 and holding. Mostly.").

4. **Issues (`issues.html`)** — Day One #1–3 with cover art, summaries (§3), and buy links; "Coming Soon" cards for #4–6 using existing covers (`Starfell_issue4 COVER A.png`, `StarFell_Issue_Five_Cover_A.png`, etc.) and pitch teasers. Optional: 4–6 page preview lightbox from `pitch/pages/07–13.png` (already-public sample pages).
5. **Gallery (`gallery.html`)** — Filterable: Covers / Concept Art / Behind the Scenes. Covers folder + Concept Art/Final + selected misc images (interior excerpts, `issue_4_inks.png` as process art).
6. **About (`about.html`)** — Mike's story: Wetumpka transplant, the real 85-million-year-old impact crater, the Fain Theater/Blob spark, the bridge rebuilt bolt-by-bolt in CG, the memorial motive ("leave your mark"). Credit the full team: Mike Uhlir (creator/writer), Lucas "Lks" Assis (art), Gui/Guilherme Sabino (colors), Leo McGovern (letters), Tiffany Crook (edits), guest cover artist Will Robson. Link the Alabama Ramble interview and CG bridge intro video (https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=eNbyQ4A5AuE).
7. **Contact** — section on About or standalone: mike@starfellstudios.com, socials, press kit link (the pitch PDF could be offered to press/retailers on request rather than posted).

Build notes: single CSS file, semantic HTML, lazy-load gallery images, generate web-sized derivatives (most source art is 5–8 MB; target ≤300 KB WebP for page use, full-res only in lightbox). Site must work as a pure static build (no backend) for GitHub Pages.

**Real-world hooks (About page):** StarFell is modeled on Wetumpka, AL — site of a real ~85-million-year-old impact crater ("one of the rarest above-ground impact craters in the world"). Fat Lance is named for Mike's childhood best friend. Mike built a bolt-accurate CG model of the bridge during the pandemic for the animated intro.

**Key dates:** Meteor falls ~60,000 years ago (in-story). Jack Levins dies Sept 18, 1956. Day One: Saturday, October 20, 1962. World Shakers frame story: October 27, 1962.
